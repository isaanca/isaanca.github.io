STEM Escape Rooms 2019

Our STEM escape rooms were developed by club members, centered around the lives of famous women in STEM and their accomplishments. They are our largest event to date, an after-school event for Nueva upper school students. Students and faculty came together to solve puzzles and learn about the lives of incredible women in STEM!

Want to sign up for this event?

Participants will be faced with the following puzzles:

May 16: Chemistry

Created by Claire G, Maya M, Aidan P, Evan S, and Annie Z

Lise Meitner has discovered nuclear fission, but her male colleague is going to receive all of the credit for it! It is your job to send Lise Meitner's papers documenting her discovery to the Nobel Prize committee, so that she can get the recognition she deserves. However, the papers with her work are stuck in a locked box! Can you use Meitner's lab notes and your knowledge of chemistry to crack the code before the award ceremony?

May 23: Cryptology

Created by Kate E, Osher L, and Merritt V

Expert code-breakers, you have been summoned by the court to settle a dispute. A mysterious ship was sunk by the US Coast Guard for refusing to be searched. Canada is suing the Coast Guard for damages for the ship, its cargo, and personnel losses. Can you help Elizebeth Smith Friedman crack the codes and find the truth behind the ship?

May 30: Electrical Engineering

Created by Daniel A, Daniel B, Molly D, and Tara S

There has been a blackout at Nueva, and it is your job to use your engineering skills to restore power! Using notes from electrical power system analyst Edith Clarke, the first electrical engineer to receive a MSc degree from MIT, can you unlock the power grid and restore power to Nueva?
